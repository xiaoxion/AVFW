    Hello!

    What are the 'top selling' app categories in the U.S.? Japan? Great Britain?

    U.S. (Statista, 2013) (App Brain, 2013)

    1. Games
    2. Education
    3. Entertainment
    4. Personalization
    5. Lifestyle

    Japan (Toto, 2013)

    1. Games
    2. Productivity
    3. Tools
    4. Photography
    5. Music

    Great Britain (Richmond, 2011) (Market, 2013)

    1. Games
    2. Travel
    3. Entertaiment
    4. Social
    5. Productivity

    Based on your three answers above, what might you infer about each of the markets listed?

    According to the data that I was able to gather, the U.S. and Great Britain, which are both considered western cultures, had similar tastes. Although the main difference was in how much interest they have in travel apps. Japan on the other hand has a vastly different taste, but the only constant between all cultures was games. All cultures that are somewhat industrialized tended to have an interest in games.

    What is the average cost and bandwidth limits per user (in general) in each of the three markets? How may this impact development?

    US (Digital Trends, 2012)(Maximum PC, 2012)(NewAmerica, 2013) $5.50 per GB Mobile Bandwidth Cap incremental by 2 GB on average

    Japan (Maximum PC, 2012)(NewAmerica, 2013) $4.48 per GB Mobile Bandwidth Cap incremental by 5 GB on average

    Great Britain (Digital Trends, 2012)(Maximum PC, 2012)(NewAmerica, 2013) $2.50 per GB Mobile Bandwidth Cap incremental by 1 GB on average

    This would limit development by forcing the developers to have to integrate some sort of data reducing measures. The users would be upset if the developer’s app required constant connectivity and caused them to use all their data or face data overage charges. Although this is considered best practice, it also means that they have to spend resources optimizing their network and app data usage to take this into consideration and limit the request made via their mobile network.

    And, finally, what would be your suggested strategy in project topic and global market choice? Feel free to have a first and second choice.

    Personally, I would have my app be more focused on somewhat more westernized cultures, then I would expand to include a larger array of cultures and languages. By choosing the English language first, I can reach a large percentage of the UK, US, and Australian markets in one swoop. Since they are considered western, and tend to have some of the same tastes, I wouldn’t have to change the way the app functions within each society, but rather on functionality of the actual app. Once the functionality is flushed out, I would then focus towards globalizing the app and introduce more options for less developed countries that have sparse internet connectivity.
