    Hello!

    1. Less obvious perks of using 'live' data resources include logins and payments. How could Twitter be used in these two scenarios?

    Most people that develop tend to forget about the great opportunities of integrating a non-native data from another source. Twitter offers many interesting options, such Login with Twitter. Although integrating it may actually seem like another loophole for us to have to jump through, the end user experience is heightened thus we raise the value of our site, app, and/or brand. This means more profits and happier customers that may cause more exposure for our brand.

    There are also some programs that let you “pay with a tweet”. These brands place a value on your followers and allow you to take their product for free, and use your followers and exposure to the brand to pay for it. This innovative system lets more people access your products while at the same time they expose you to a much larger demographic for more potential buyers.

    2. Visit Programmableweb.com and search for JSON-P data sources. Why would we need JSON-P, specifically?

    JSON P is very important because the technique allows us to bypass the normal limitations of having to abide to the same origin policy and pull dynamic content from other servers. Perhaps a server that is used to store and generate user generate content is stored on another server in another network, this technique would allow us to pull from that server. Also this allows us to pull from other services, such as public API’s, that allow us to pull in content, such as social feed, from other sites entirely. Both enriching our platform and pleasing the user.

    3. Based on the JSON-P search results: what data resource(s) do you think might pair well with which types of Native device features?

    The most useful data resource I could think of is using notifications with the Native App. It is the most useful feature as the user doesn’t have to constantly check the app when there is some new dynamic content pushed from the site.

    But that is boring and very common. I think an interesting way to integrate the native features with dynamic content would be to use Google Maps API and integrate a user defined overlay. The JSON data would by dynamically created on Googles end, in terms of the maps and the overlay. But also the user’s content would be the forefront of the system.

    4. Please describe any design and functionality intentions you may have for your Demo app components this month.

    I believe that an app should be simple and should be minimal in how the design is. I want the content to be the forefront of the interface and the design to literally just fade away to the background, yet be easily distinguishable. I don’t think I have the time to integrate animations to accomplish this in this short month. But I think I can make the design clean, minimal, and also pleasant to look at.

    Functionally speaking, I want the app to be quick and to the point. The idea of the app is that users can quickly and efficiently define work and organize their thoughts while on the road. If I make too many requirements or I make the interface too clunky, then the user may choose to avoid my app whereas if I do not ask enough, they may not input enough information for it to be useful to them. Basically I want them to spend the least amount of time in my app, but to spend more resources on being a more efficient business owner.

    5. Briefly describe some past 'lessons learned' regarding workflow as it relates to research, design, development, etc.

    Well, I am kind of quick to assume that my ideas can span what the majority thinks. So something that I do now is if/when I have an idea, I consult with other people. For instance, I had a friend that proposed an app idea to me, I thought it was the stupidest thing in the world. But I asked around and found that over 87% of my friends were interested and over 70% mentioned they would actually download it. My new workflow involves trying to get more opinions and doing more research to see what the majority is interested in and what the user wants. Not just what I think is convenient or right.
